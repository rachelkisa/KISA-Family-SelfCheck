A About Your Child

Seven Everyday Signs

For each statement, choose how often it describes your child right now — not how you hope they'll be.

1. My child shows curiosity and picks up new ideas quickly for their age (emerging giftedness).

2. My child approaches new material with genuine curiosity, even when it's difficult (eager to learn).

3. My child cooperates well in group activities and follows shared routines and manners (socially ready).

4. My child brings a constructive, imaginative attitude to problem-solving (positive & creative).

5. My child doesn't give up quickly when something is hard, and works through frustration on their own (resilient).

6. My child feels proud of their own progress, more than they need to compare themselves with others (self-motivated).

7. My child enjoys learning alongside other children, celebrating a peer's success and working together with patience and respect, rather than needing to outdo them (collaborative spirit).

B Growing Into the 2-Year Advanced Level

Ready to Reach a Little Further?

KISA's curriculum reaches about two years beyond what's typically expected at this age — an exciting stretch, not a hurdle. These reflections help you spot the everyday strengths your child already brings, and where a bit of encouragement will help them shine.

1. My child can stay engaged through a full day of varied classes, especially when something catches their interest.

2. My child enjoys exploring reading or ideas that stretch a little beyond what's typical for their age.

3. When my child struggles with something hard, they're usually willing to give it another try.

4. My child is building good independence with homework and practice, needing a little less hands-on help over time.

5. My child lights up when offered something a bit harder, once the current work starts to feel easy.

6. My child is growing steadily in English expression, and is comfortable stretching toward content above what's typical for their age.

C About Your Family

Three Household Values

KISA families partner closely with teachers — these reflect the values behind that partnership.

1. We value our child's emotional and social growth as much as their academic achievement.

2. We're willing to engage with the wider KISA community, not just our own child's group.

3. We tend to respond to setbacks with patience and encouragement rather than pressure.

D Growing With the KISA Community

Everyday Habits That Make It Click

KISA feels best when families and teachers move in the same rhythm. These reflect the everyday habits that help that partnership feel natural.

1. We're ready to settle into a steady daily rhythm around KISA's schedule (8:10 arrival, 3:05 dismissal) throughout the program year.

2. We're comfortable embracing KISA's shared routines — like the no photo/video policy, uniform, and shuttle plans — as simply part of being in this community together.

3. We trust KISA's teachers and processes, and prefer to work things through with KISA directly whenever a question comes up.

4. We're looking forward to being part of family sessions — Curriculum Sessions, PTC Week, orientation — as a way to stay closely connected.

5. We're excited for our child to grow through both Korean-style rigor and an English-medium, internationally minded classroom, and we'll nurture that balance at home.
